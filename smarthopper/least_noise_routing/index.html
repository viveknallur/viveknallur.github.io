Screenshots of SmartHopper in Action

SmartHopper is able to use sensor data, gathered from smartphones, and integrate them with maps. This allows citizens to make queries other than the usual fastest or shortest routes between two points. Citizens can also now ask for the Least Noisy Route. The fact that the noise data is collected by the citizens, in the first place, closes the loop between participatory sensing (where data is voluntarily collected by participants) and the people that live in the city1.

When you first start the SmartHopper software, it looks pretty much like any other map/routing software

But then when you look for the Least Noisy Route, it searches through the noise data collected by the smartphones, and calculates the least noisy routes. There’s also an overlay, that allows you to visualize the noise over all the streets in the city.

If you look carefully, you’ll notice that it’s not just an overlay, but the suggested route has also changed!

Other Uses

Noise is only one of the sensors that is available in a city. The uses that SmartHopper can be put to, are limited only by your imagination and the availability of data. If you have a particular use-case in mind, or if you would like to use SmartHopper for your own city, get in touch
